# Supplier Analysis & Cost Optimization

## Context
In my current role, I work with purchasing processes involving different international suppliers such as USA, Germany, Japan, and South Korea. 

While working with this data, I noticed that prices, consumption patterns, and supplier selection can vary significantly, which may lead to inefficiencies or missed opportunities to optimize costs.

This project is based on that real-world context.

---

## 🎯 Objective
The goal of this analysis is to better understand purchasing behavior and identify patterns that could help improve supplier selection and reduce overall costs.

---

## 📂 Data
For this project, I used a simulated dataset that reflects real purchasing scenarios, including:

- Supplier (country or subsidiary)
- Customer
- Quantity purchased
- Unit price
- Order date

---

## 🔍 Approach
I approached this project as a business problem rather than just a technical exercise.

The analysis includes:

- Exploring price differences between suppliers  
- Analyzing purchasing volume  
- Evaluating total cost by supplier  
- Understanding customer consumption patterns  

---

## 📊 Key Findings
- Some suppliers consistently show higher average prices  
- Purchasing volume is not always aligned with the most cost-efficient supplier  
- A small number of customers represent a large portion of total spending  

---

## 💡 Insights
From the analysis, it became clear that:

- There are opportunities to optimize supplier selection  
- Price variability between suppliers could be used as a negotiation point  
- Understanding consumption patterns can support better purchasing decisions  

---

## 📈 Business Impact
This type of analysis can help:

- Reduce purchasing costs  
- Improve supplier selection strategies  
- Support data-driven decision making in procurement  

---

## 💭 Thought Process
While working on this project, I tried to focus not only on the technical side, but also on how this analysis would be used in a real business environment.

Coming from a role where I interact with purchasing and sales data, I wanted to make sure the insights were practical and aligned with real decision-making processes.

---

## 🛠️ Tools Used
- Python

---

## ✅ Conclusion
This analysis helped me better understand how purchasing decisions impact overall costs. 

By comparing suppliers, prices, and consumption patterns, it becomes clear that small changes in supplier selection can lead to meaningful cost improvements.

This type of approach can be useful in real business scenarios where data-driven decisions are needed to optimize operations.
